Zermatt

The highest winter sports area in the Alps, with ski terrain at altitudes between 2,500 and 3,900 meters and more than 2,133 meters of vertical drop - Switzerland's largest - it seems almost unfair that Zermatt should also have Europe's most impressive mountain.

St. Moritz

One of the oldest and most famous ski resorts in the world, St. Moritz twice hosted the Winter Olympics, in 1928 and 1948, and the Olympic ski jump and slopes are often the site of world skiing events, but St. Moritz is not only for experts and Olympians. Its more than 20 lifts transport skiers to terrain for all skill levels, and it is known for some of the best intermediates in Switzerland, with several very long intermediates.

Davos-Klosters

One of Europe's largest winter sports areas and one of the highest altitudes, Davos is a series of ski resorts that line several miles along the Landwasser River Valley in eastern Switzerland. The two main centers are the completely different towns of Davos and Klosters; for an alpine country feel, choose Klosters as a base, but multiple ski areas are interconnected, so it's easy to ski any combination. Together, they offer more than 300 kilometers of groomed trails, most of which are classified as intermediate. But the incredible off-piste terrain attracts a loyal following of experts.
